About
Me

I am a PhD researcher at the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee, working at the intersection of quantum information theory and practical quantum communication systems. My research centres on the theoretical design and security analysis of Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) protocols — with emphasis on finite-key analysis, device-independent frameworks, and entanglement-based schemes.

My academic journey began with a BSc in Physics from Shivaji College, followed by an MSc from SRTM University and an M.Tech in Quantum Technology from IIT Jodhpur. This interdisciplinary training gave me a robust foundation spanning quantum optics, quantum information, and mathematical physics.

I am particularly motivated by the challenge of bridging the gap between theoretical quantum protocols and their real-world deployment in noise-tolerant, scalable quantum networks. Beyond research, I'm passionate about quantum education and open-source quantum simulation tools.
